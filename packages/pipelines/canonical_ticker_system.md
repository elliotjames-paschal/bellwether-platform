# Bellwether Canonical Ticker System

## Overview

Every political prediction market gets a **two-level canonical ticker** generated by GPT-4o:

- **`event_ticker`** — identifies the question/event family (coarse grain)
- **`market_ticker`** — identifies the specific outcome within that event (fine grain)

Two markets on different platforms match if and only if they produce the **same `market_ticker`**.

Markets that share an `event_ticker` but differ in `market_ticker` are siblings (e.g., "Dems win NC Senate" and "GOP win NC Senate" are siblings under the same event).

---

## Ticker Format

```
event_ticker:  {CATEGORY}-{TOPIC}-{SCOPE}-{YEAR}
market_ticker: {CATEGORY}-{TOPIC}-{SCOPE}-{VARIANT}-{YEAR}
```

- All UPPERCASE
- Segments separated by `-`
- No spaces, no special characters
- Names normalized to LASTNAME (or LASTNAME_FIRSTNAME if ambiguous)
- All years are 4-digit

## Category Taxonomy

### 1. ELEC — Elections & Partisan Control

Who wins an election or controls a legislative body.

```
event_ticker:  ELEC-{OFFICE}-{JURISDICTION}-{YEAR}
market_ticker: ELEC-{OFFICE}-{JURISDICTION}-{VARIANT}-{YEAR}
```

**OFFICE values:**
- `PRES` — presidential
- `PRES_PRIMARY` — presidential primary
- `SENATE` — US Senate seat
- `HOUSE` — US House (chamber control)
- `HOUSE_SEAT` — specific House district
- `GOV` — governor
- `MAYOR` — mayor
- `PM` — prime minister
- `PARLIAMENT` — parliamentary
- `LEADER` — generic head of state

**JURISDICTION:** ISO country code or US state abbreviation
- US states: `US_NC`, `US_AL`, `US_CA`, etc.
- Countries: `US`, `UK`, `FR`, `DE`, `CO`, `KR`, etc.
- `US` alone for national-level (president, chamber control)

**VARIANT** (what makes markets within the same event unique):
- Party: `DEM`, `GOP`, `LAB`, `CON`, etc.
- Candidate: `TRUMP`, `HARRIS`, `OCASIO_CORTEZ`
- For chamber control: party is the variant

**Examples:**
```
Kalshi:  "Will the Democratic party win the House?"
  event_ticker:  ELEC-HOUSE-US-2026
  market_ticker: ELEC-HOUSE-US-DEM-2026

Polymarket: "Will the Republican Party control the Senate after the 2026 Midterm?"
  event_ticker:  ELEC-SENATE-US-2026
  market_ticker: ELEC-SENATE-US-GOP-2026

Kalshi:  "Will the Democratic party win the governorship in Alabama"
  event_ticker:  ELEC-GOV-US_AL-2026
  market_ticker: ELEC-GOV-US_AL-DEM-2026

Polymarket: "Will Yoo Jeong-bok win the 2026 Incheon mayoral election"
  event_ticker:  ELEC-MAYOR-KR_INCHEON-2026
  market_ticker: ELEC-MAYOR-KR_INCHEON-YOO-2026
```

## Normalization Rules

### Person Names
- Use LASTNAME only: `TRUMP`, `BIDEN`, `POWELL`, `MUSK`
- If ambiguous (two people with same last name), use LASTNAME_FIRSTNAME: `OCASIO_CORTEZ`, `KENNEDY_ROBERT`
- Remove accents: `CEPEDA`, `MELENCHON`, `LULA`
- Spaces → underscores: `OCASIO_CORTEZ`, `EL_SISI`

### Parties
- `DEM` — Democratic / Democrat / Democrats / Democratic Party
- `GOP` — Republican / Republicans / Republican Party / GOP
- `LAB` — Labour / Labor
- `CON` — Conservative / Tory / Tories
- `GRN` — Green
- `LIB` — Liberal / Liberal Democrat
- `IND` — Independent
- Other parties: abbreviated 3-letter code

### US States
Always prefix with `US_`: `US_NC`, `US_CA`, `US_TX`
Use standard 2-letter postal abbreviations.

### Countries
Use ISO 3166-1 alpha-2: `US`, `UK`, `FR`, `DE`, `UA`, `RU`, `CN`, `KR`, `JP`, `IL`, `PS`, `CO`, `AR`, `BR`
Exception: Use `UK` not `GB`.

### Offices
- `PRES` — President
- `PM` — Prime Minister
- `GOV` — Governor
- `SENATE` — Senate seat
- `HOUSE` — House (chamber)
- `HOUSE_SEAT` — specific House district
- `MAYOR` — Mayor
- `PARLIAMENT` — Parliament / National Assembly

### Thresholds
- Percentages: `GT_1.0PCT`, `LT_40PCT`, `HIT_35PCT`
- Dollar amounts: `GT_38T`, `LT_21000`
- Basis points: `GT_25BPS`
- Days: `GTE_7D`, `GTE_30D`
- Counts: `GT_500`, `GTE_40`
- Direction prefixes: `GT_` (greater than), `LT_` (less than), `GTE_` (greater than or equal), `HIT_` (reaches exactly)

### Time
- **CRITICAL: derive year from `end_date` field, not question phrasing**
- "in 2025?" with end_date 2025-12-31 → `2025`
- "before 2026?" with end_date 2025-12-31 → `2025` (same market!)
- If end_date falls in January of year Y, the market likely refers to year Y-1
- Rule of thumb: `year = end_date[:4]`, but if end_date is Jan 1-31, use `year = end_date_year - 1`
- Quarter: `2026_Q3`
- Month: `JUN2025`, `OCT2025`
- If market says "during Trump administration" with end_date 2029-01-20 → `2029`
- If market says "in his/her lifetime" → `2099`

## Critical Matching Rules

1. **Same concept = same ticker, regardless of phrasing.** "Will Democrats win the House?" and "Will the Democratic Party control the House after 2026 Midterms?" MUST produce the same ticker.

2. **Standalone binary markets get `ANY` as their variant.** "US recession in 2025?" has variant `ANY` because it's not part of a multi-outcome group.

3. **Grouped markets use the distinguishing attribute as variant.** In "Which party wins the House?" the variant is the party. In "Who will be first to leave the cabinet?" the variant is the person.

4. **Use `end_date` for year, not question phrasing.** "Macron out in 2025?" (end_date 2025-12-31) and "Macron out before 2026?" (end_date 2025-12-31) MUST produce the same year. Rule: `year = end_date[:4]`, unless end_date is Jan 1-31, then `year = end_date_year - 1`.

5. **Use the most specific year derivable from context.** If the question says "in 2025" use `2025`. Prefer end_date over question text for year extraction.

6. **Normalize aggressively.** "Federal Reserve" = "the Fed" = "Fed" → `FED`. "marijuana" = "weed" = "cannabis" → `MARIJUANA`. "ceasefire" = "peace deal" (if same conflict) should still match.

7. **When in doubt, prefer broader categories.** If a market about tariffs could be LEGIS or REGULATE, pick whichever category its political_category suggests. The category assignment is pre-labeled and should be respected.

```json
{
  "event_ticker": "ELEC-HOUSE-US-2026",
  "market_ticker": "ELEC-HOUSE-US-DEM-2026",
  "category": "ELEC",
  "confidence": "high"

```

`confidence` is `high`, `medium`, or `low` — flags markets where the LLM wasn't sure about normalization.

## Batching Strategy

Send markets in batches of ~50 to GPT-4o. Include the full taxonomy above as the system prompt. Each user message contains 50 market payloads, and GPT-4o returns 50 ticker assignments.

Estimated cost at GPT-4o rates ($2.50/1M input, $10/1M output):
- System prompt: ~4k tokens × 1 per batch
- 50 markets per batch: ~15k input tokens, ~3k output tokens
- 13,108 markets ÷ 50 = ~263 batches
- Total: ~263 × (19k × $2.50/1M + 3k × $10/1M) ≈ **$20-25**

Alternatively with batch API (50% discount): **~$10-12**

## GPT-4o System Prompt

```
You are a prediction market classifier. Your job is to assign canonical tickers to prediction markets so that markets asking the same question on different platforms produce identical tickers.

## Your Task

For each market, output:
- `event_ticker`: identifies the question family (coarse — groups related markets)
- `market_ticker`: identifies the specific outcome (fine — unique per market)
- `confidence`: "high", "medium", or "low"

Two markets MATCH if they have the same `market_ticker`. Markets sharing an `event_ticker` but different `market_ticker` are siblings (same event, different outcomes).

## Ticker Format

event_ticker:  {CATEGORY}-{...segments...}-{YEAR}
market_ticker: {CATEGORY}-{...segments...}-{VARIANT}-{YEAR}

Rules:
- ALL UPPERCASE, segments separated by `-`
- No spaces (use `_` within a segment: `OCASIO_CORTEZ`, `TAX_CUT`)
- Person names: LASTNAME only (or LASTNAME_FIRSTNAME if ambiguous). Remove accents.
- Years: always 4 digits. "Before 2027" → 2027. "During Trump admin" → 2029. "Lifetime" → 2099.
- Standalone markets (not part of a group) use `ANY` as variant.

## Normalization Tables

**Parties:** Democratic/Democrat/Democrats → `DEM` | Republican/Republicans/GOP → `GOP` | Labour → `LAB` | Conservative/Tory → `CON` | Green → `GRN` | Liberal → `LIB` | Independent → `IND`

**US States:** Always `US_{2-letter}`: US_NC, US_CA, US_TX, US_AL, etc.

**Countries:** ISO alpha-2: US, UK, FR, DE, UA, RU, CN, KR, JP, IL, PS, CO, AR, BR, MX, etc. Use UK not GB.

**Offices:** PRES, PM, GOV, SENATE, HOUSE, HOUSE_SEAT, MAYOR, PARLIAMENT, LEADER

**Central Banks:** Federal Reserve/the Fed → `FED` | ECB, BOE, BOJ, BOC, RBA

**Thresholds:** GT_ (>), LT_ (<), GTE_ (>=), HIT_ (reaches). Examples: GT_1.0PCT, LT_38T, GTE_7D, HIT_40PCT, GT_25BPS

**Common synonyms that MUST normalize identically:**
- weed / marijuana / cannabis → `MARIJUANA`
- ceasefire / peace deal (same conflict) → `CEASEFIRE`
- government shutdown / shutdown → `SHUTDOWN`
- national debt / US debt → `DEBT_LEVEL`
- Fed rate cut / FOMC rate cut / Federal Reserve rate reduction → `RATE_CUT`

## Critical Rules

1. **MATCHING IS THE GOAL.** Two markets asking the same question on different platforms MUST get the same ticker. Prefer consistency over precision.

2. **Use structured fields first.** `custom_strike`, `groupItemTitle`, `event_title` are more reliable than parsing the question text.

3. **YEAR: always derive from `end_date` field, NOT question phrasing.** This is critical. "Macron out in 2025?" (end_date: 2025-12-31) and "Will Macron leave before 2026?" (end_date: 2025-12-31) are the SAME market. Extract the year from end_date. If end_date is January 1 or January 20 of year Y, the market likely refers to year Y-1 (e.g., end_date 2027-01-20 for a "during Trump admin" market → use 2029 only if it truly spans the full term, otherwise extract the calendar year from end_date). Rule of thumb: `year = end_date[:4]` unless end_date is Jan 1-31, in which case `year = int(end_date[:4]) - 1`.

4. **Standalone binary markets get variant `ANY`.** If there's only one market (not part of a group), variant = `ANY`.

5. **Grouped markets: variant = the distinguishing attribute.** Party for elections, person name for "who will leave cabinet", threshold for "will GDP exceed X%".

6. **Normalize synonyms aggressively.** The same real-world event phrased differently MUST produce the same ticker.

7. **When a market doesn't cleanly fit a template, do your best and set confidence to "low".**
```
